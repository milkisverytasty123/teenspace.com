The moon is less boring than you think. In conclusion, the moon play an important role for the Earth.

FORMATION

Now the most important thing to know before you introduce your self to some facts about the moon, is to know how it was formed. Long ago; around 4 billion years, the earth was still just born, what is most widely accepted today is the giant-impact theory. It proposes that the Moon formed during a collision between the Earth and another small planet, about the size of Mars. The debris from this impact collected in an orbit around Earth to form the Moon.

moon's effect on Earth's rotation speed:

Earth's rotation speed just right for our fellas. But as the earth rotates, the bulge raised on its surface by the Moon's gravity is always a little ahead of the Moon rather than directly under it. The Moon pulls back on the bulge, and in the process it slows the whole planet down.

SALTY OCEANS:

How oceans became salty is a pretty easy question to be answered,Salt in the ocean comes from two sources: runoff from the land and openings in the seafloor. Rocks on land are the major source of salts dissolved in seawater. Rainwater that falls on land is slightly acidic, so it erodes rocks.

Volcano Eruptions:

Volcanoes erupt when molten rock called magma rises to the surface. Magma is formed when the earth's mantle melts.
